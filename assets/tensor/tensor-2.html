Tensors

It is difficult to give a simple definition of a tensor. McConnel in his book "Applications of Tensor Analysis" gives a one sentence definition which contains the phrase "... functions ... considered in the last chapter ... ".. Weyl in his book, Space, Time and Matter requires 35 pages of discussion before he also provides a one sentence definition. In this article, none will be attempted even though the use of the word tensorwill be widespread. We can remark, however, that tensor calculas is concerned with a study of abstract quantities in geometry and physics called tensor, whose properties and relations are independent of the coordinate frame. Furthermore, a tensor is fully described if its components in any coordinate frame are given because by definition a specific kind of transformation law is used to represent the tensor in any other coordinate frame.
